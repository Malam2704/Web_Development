Well I guess you've been peaked at my educational experiences, and I guess you'll just have to read on to find the rest.

High School: The Brooklyn Technical High School

Need to Know: Well as you can see form the picture above, I went to the Brooklyn Technical High School or BTHS for short. It's one of the prestigious NYC Specialized High School, but to me it was a lot more than that. It was organized in 1922 under the Manhattan bridge's Dumbo neighborhood, but since 1933 has been at its location off of Fort Greene Park. Our school is noted as the largest Public Hignschool in America at approximately 6,500 students enrolled, and we get a lot of funding more than we should.

Date of Attendance: Sept. 2016 - June 2020

School Basics: Well the school is a STEM education based school, where for 9 and 10th grades all students were on a general education/technical track taking regular classes along with some tech related classes such as PLTW Design Drafting & Production and AP Computer Science Principles.

School Majors: We did have something special compared to other schools, school majors for 11th & 12th grades. These majors allow you to take classes based on what academic major track you choose, if you choose Social Science you'd have to take a sociology class, AP human geography and the works. We have 18 majors, and I was in a CTE major, Career and Technical education , where we would build and learn technical skills for the future. These were heavy Tech related majors such as Software ,Aerospace,Civil, Electrical Engineering, and Industrial Design,Architecture, and mine: Mechatronics and Robotics. I was a mix of computing and engineering, where would would learn programming, in the mechanical discipline making robots, and controlling automated machines with code.

Bonuses: I was also in many clubs and organizations, because the school has over 160 to off its students. I joined various clubs such as, the SAT club, the Soccer club,the South Asian culture club, the Muslim students Association, and my all time favorite, the CHESS club, these clubs all had phenomenal membership, but the chess club came in at it's 2018 peak of 452 students, wow! I 'd visit some other clubs but would never consider myself a member.

College: The Rochester Institute of Technology

School Basics: The Rochester Institute of Technology is a private university in the greater Rochester are of NY, not Minnesota! It has a current undergraduate enrollment of approximated 13,500 students, and it's renown for it's Golisano College of Computing, with it's amazing stats including the first Software Engineering program in the U.S., the 95% job placement rate, 25th best college in securing jobs, and etc.

Where I am: So I am currently a freshman at the Computing Exploration Major at RIT , an undecided major for student looking to get into the computing field, but can't decide between majors such as Computing Information Technology, Computer Science, Computing Security, Web and Mobile Computing, Software Engineering, Human Centered Computing, and New Media and Interactive Development.

Where I see myself: I am currently interested in the Web and Mobile Computing Major here at RIT. I'm just fascinated about the wide varieties of tasks done by the many types of developers, and I would love to see myself as a Full Stack developer in the future.
